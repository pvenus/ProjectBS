# Story Image Element Guide

## Purpose

Define what story images should be built from.

This guide controls historical period inference, environment, architecture,
settlement, terrain, weather, materials, props, social class, everyday life, and
element avoid rules. It does not define composition, camera, lighting, color, or
rendering style. Use `StoryImageVisualGuide.md` for those.

## Historical Period

- Infer the period from story context instead of applying a generic Joseon-style
  surface treatment.
- Use historically grounded Korean period details when the story context points
  there.
- Let era cues appear through practical objects, construction methods,
  clothing, tools, road conditions, lighting sources, and social behavior.
- Do not add famous historical icons unless the story specifically requires
  them.

## Environment

- Infer the environment from the event location, journey state, local economy,
  danger level, season, and weather.
- Build places as lived-in settings with practical traces of use.
- Show whether the place is rural, mountain, forest, roadside, market, shrine,
  noble estate, poor village, military route, ruined settlement, river crossing,
  or plague-struck area when context supports it.
- Every environmental detail should support the current story moment.

## Architecture

- Choose architecture from social class, region, and function.
- Use wood posts, earthen walls, thatch, tile roof, stone foundation, hanji
  doors, rough fences, storage sheds, gates, shrine structures, or roadside
  shelters when appropriate.
- Keep buildings practical, weathered, and integrated with the terrain.
- Avoid ornamental fantasy architecture unless the story explicitly calls for a
  supernatural place.

## Settlement

- Reflect settlement scale and condition:
  - isolated house
  - hamlet
  - poor village
  - market street
  - official road station
  - shrine compound
  - abandoned camp
  - damaged or plague-struck community
- Show everyday traces such as footpaths, storage jars, firewood, drying cloth,
  rope, baskets, farming tools, ritual remnants, or repair marks when relevant.

## Terrain

- Infer terrain from travel context and location:
  - muddy road
  - mountain pass
  - pine forest
  - rocky slope
  - riverbank
  - field edge
  - courtyard
  - ruined path
  - snow, dust, wet ground, or dry grass
- Terrain should affect object placement, footprints, stains, damage, posture,
  and movement clues.

## Weather

- Use weather as a story element, not decoration.
- Rain, fog, snow, dust, wind, humidity, harsh sun, or night cold should affect
  materials, visibility, clothing, footprints, fire, paper, mud, and mood.
- Do not add dramatic weather if it contradicts the event or distracts from the
  key clue.

## Materials

- Prefer weathered, imperfect, handmade materials:
  - rough wood
  - straw
  - hemp
  - bamboo
  - earthenware
  - aged hanji
  - rough cloth
  - rope
  - stone
  - mud
  - ash
  - worn leather
  - tarnished metal
- Materials should show use, damage, repair, dirt, moisture, age, or social
  condition when relevant.

## Props

- Use only practical, story-relevant props.
- Props should reveal action, status, danger, loss, poverty, ritual, travel,
  labor, or battle aftermath.
- Strong prop categories include:
  - tools
  - vessels
  - charms
  - damaged weapons
  - medicine bundles
  - rope
  - baskets
  - torn cloth
  - footprints
  - blood traces
  - monster traces
  - ritual fragments
  - broken doors
  - abandoned food or supplies
- Every prop should support the key clue or core situation.

## Social Class

- Infer social class from location, role, clothing condition, tools, food,
  building quality, storage, footwear, and posture.
- Poor commoners, travelers, shamans, officials, soldiers, bandits, merchants,
  servants, and nobles should be distinguished through practical details rather
  than labels.
- Avoid making all people or spaces look equally clean, wealthy, or decorative.

## Everyday Life

- Add everyday-life details when they clarify the event:
  - cooking traces
  - sleeping mats
  - laundry
  - farming tools
  - firewood
  - jars
  - animal pens
  - road dust
  - repair patches
  - worn thresholds
  - storage bundles
  - handwoven fabric
- These details should feel natural, not staged.
- Do not add unrelated objects just to fill the frame.

## Element Avoid

Avoid these content choices:

- Modern objects.
- Factory-made appearance.
- Decorative fantasy props.
- Clean manufactured materials.
- Story-irrelevant objects.
- Iconic historical cliches used without story need.
- Generic palace, royal, or noble imagery when the event does not require it.
- Clean costume-drama surfaces with no use, dirt, age, or repair.
- Readable text used as decoration.
- Objects that explain the plot too literally.
- Props that compete with the key clue.
